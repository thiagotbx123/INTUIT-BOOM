# 1. WELCOME & OVERVIEW

## What is a TSA at TestBox?

A Technical Solutions Architect (TSA) is the bridge between our customers and our product. You'll lead discovery sessions, design demo environments, configure realistic test scenarios, and ensure our customers get the most out of TestBox.

## Your First 90 Days at a Glance

| Phase | Focus | Key Outcome |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Week 1** | Company culture, tools, meet the team | Feel at home, accounts set up |
| **Week 2** | Product deep dive, shadow sessions | Understand TestBox inside out |
| **Week 3-4** | Hands-on practice, first customer assist | Deliver first contribution |
| **Day 30** | Independent on basic tasks | Handle simple requests solo |
| **Day 60** | Own customer relationships | Lead discovery calls |
| **Day 90** | Full autonomy | Manage environments end-to-end |

# APPENDIX B: BUDDY PROGRAM GUIDELINES

## For the Buddy

### Your Role
You're the new TSA's go-to person for questions, context, and support during their first 30 days. Think of yourself as a friendly guide, not a trainer.

### Time Commitment
- Week 1: ~2 hours/day
- Week 2: ~1 hour/day
- Week 3-4: ~30 min/day
- After: As needed

### Key Responsibilities

1. **Be Available**
   - Respond to Slack messages within 30 min during work hours
   - Schedule daily check-ins for Week 1
   - Let them know your calendar/availability

2. **Provide Context**
   - Explain "how things really work" beyond documentation
   - Share unwritten norms and culture tips
   - Introduce them to people informally

3. **Answer Questions**
   - No question is too small
   - If you don't know, help find who does
   - Encourage them to ask

4. **Give Feedback**
   - Be honest but kind
   - Celebrate small wins
   - Share observations with manager (helpful context, not criticism)

### Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Be patient | Rush them |
| Share your own early struggles | Make them feel dumb |
| Check in proactively | Wait for them to ask |
| Celebrate progress | Compare to others |
| Be honest about challenges | Sugarcoat everything |
