# TSA CROSS-TEAM INTEGRATION PLAN
## Building Relationships Across TestBox

---

# OVERVIEW

As a TSA, you're at the intersection of customers, product, and engineering. Strong cross-functional relationships are essential to your success - and to TestBox's.

This guide outlines how to build effective working relationships with every team you'll interact with.

# TEAM PROFILES & INTERACTION GUIDE

---

## 1. ENGINEERING TEAM

### Team Overview
- **Size:** ~20 people
- **Structure:** Platform, Product, Infrastructure squads
- **Work Style:** Sprint-based, Linear-driven
- **Key Channel:** #engineering

### What They Do
- Build and maintain the TestBox platform
- Handle Capture, Convert, Compare functionality
- Manage infrastructure and reliability
- Implement new features and fix bugs

### How TSAs Work With Engineering

| TSA Need | Engineering Support | How to Request |
|----------|---------------------|----------------|
| Bug report | Investigation & fix | Linear ticket |
| Feature request | Roadmap consideration | RFC document |
| Technical question | Guidance/explanation | Slack / Office Hours |
| Customer escalation | Priority support | Escalation process |
| Demo environment issue | Troubleshooting | Slack â†’ ticket if complex |

### Communication Guidelines

**DO:**
- Provide detailed reproduction steps for bugs
- Include screenshots and logs
- Tag relevant squad/person
- Set appropriate priority
- Follow up if urgent

**DON'T:**
- DM engineers for non-urgent issues
- Expect immediate responses to tickets
- Skip the ticket process for "quick" fixes
- Escalate without trying to troubleshoot first

### Key Meetings
| Meeting | When | Purpose | TSA Role |
|---------|------|---------|----------|
| Engineering Standup | Daily 9:15 AM | Team sync | Attend if relevant issue |
| Sprint Planning | Monday 10 AM | Plan work | Share customer priorities |
| Demo Day | Friday 3 PM | Show progress | Provide feedback |

### Onboarding Touchpoints
| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1 | Meet Engineering Lead (intro call) |
| 2 | Architecture overview session |
| 2 | Attend Demo Day |
| 3 | Shadow engineering-customer handoff |
| 4 | File first bug/feature request |

### Relationship Building Tips
- Attend Demo Day regularly - engineers love showing their work
- When thanking engineers, be specific about impact ("This fix saved 3 customer calls!")
- Bring customer context to technical discussions
- Learn enough technical vocabulary to communicate clearly
- Buy them coffee/lunch when they help you out of a jam

## 2. PRODUCT TEAM

### Team Overview
- **Size:** 3-4 people
- **Structure:** PM + Design + Research
- **Work Style:** Discovery-driven, user-focused
- **Key Channel:** #product

### What They Do
- Define product vision and roadmap
- Prioritize features and improvements
- Conduct user research
- Design user experiences

### How TSAs Work With Product

| TSA Need | Product Support | How to Request |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Roadmap question | Context/timeline | Slack or 1:1 |
| Feature feedback | Input collection | Product channel or RFC |
| Customer request | Prioritization input | Structured feedback |
| Design question | UX guidance | Product office hours |
| Beta program | Coordination | Product manager |

### Communication Guidelines

**DO:**
- Share customer feedback regularly (not just complaints!)
- Quantify impact when possible ("5 customers asked for this")
- Understand roadmap constraints
- Provide context, not just requests

**DON'T:**
- Promise customers features without checking
- Bypass PM to go straight to engineering
- Assume silence means "no"
- Only share negative feedback

### Key Meetings
| Meeting | When | Purpose | TSA Role |
|---------|------|---------|----------|
| Product Sync | Wednesday 11 AM | Cross-team alignment | Share customer voice |
| Roadmap Review | Monthly | Plan review | Provide input |
| User Research Share | Bi-weekly | Insights sharing | Learn patterns |

### Onboarding Touchpoints
| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1 | Meet Product Lead (intro call) |
| 2 | Product roadmap overview |
| 3 | Shadow user research session |
| 4 | Present customer feedback |

### Relationship Building Tips
- Product loves data - bring numbers and quotes
- Understand the "why" behind roadmap decisions
- Share both problems AND potential solutions
- Become their window into customer reality
- Celebrate when customer-requested features ship

- **Work Style:** Deal-driven, fast-paced
- **Key Channel:** #sales

### What They Do

- Conduct sales demos and negotiations
- Build customer relationships (pre-sale)

|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Customer context | Deal background | Salesforce + Slack |
| Demo support | Joint presentation | Pre-scheduled |

# NETWORKING TIPS FOR NEW TSAS

## Coffee Chat Template

**Goal:** Build rapport, understand their world, find collaboration points

**Questions to Ask:**
1. What's your role and how long have you been at TestBox?
2. What does a typical week look like for you?
3. What's the best way for TSAs to work with your team?
4. What do you wish TSAs knew about your work?
5. What are you most excited about right now?

**Share About Yourself:**
- Your background briefly
- What you're learning
- How you hope to contribute

## Making Yourself Known

1. **Be present in channels** - React, respond, contribute
2. **Share wins publicly** - Celebrate customer successes
3. **Ask good questions** - Shows engagement
4. **Help others** - Even small assists build reputation
5. **Join optional events** - Team socials, demo days, etc.

## Building Trust Across Teams

| Action | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Follow through on commitments | Reliability = trust |
| Be responsive | Shows respect for others' time |
| Give credit generously | Makes people want to help you |
| Admit when you don't know | Honesty builds respect |
| Share context, not just requests | Helps others help you |

# CROSS-TEAM MEETING ETIQUETTE

## Joining Another Team's Meeting

**Before:**
- Ask if it's okay to attend
- Understand the meeting purpose
- Have specific questions or contributions

**During:**
- Introduce yourself briefly if needed
- Listen more than talk (at first)
- Take notes
- Respect their meeting norms

**After:**
- Thank the organizer
- Follow up on anything relevant
- Don't overstay your welcome

## Requesting Cross-Team Time

**Good Request:**
```
Hi [Name],

I'm [Your name], new TSA on the Solutions team. I'm trying to
learn more about [topic] and [Person who suggested] recommended
talking to you.

Would you have 20-30 minutes for a quick chat this week or next?

Happy to work around your schedule.

Thanks!
```

**What to Avoid:**
- Demanding immediate time
- Not explaining why
- Requesting too much time
- Not being flexible
